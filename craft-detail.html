The Indigo Mesa Throw

Woven by the hands of third-generation artisans in the highlands of Oaxaca, this heirloom-quality throw captures the depth of natural indigo dyeing traditions passed down through centuries. Each piece begins with hand-harvested cotton, spun on a drop spindle, and dyed in small fermentation vats before being meticulously woven on a backstrap loom. The result is a textile alive with subtle variations — a quiet testament to slow craft and the beauty of the human hand.

The Maker

Guardians of Generational Craft

For over six generations, the women of the Sahyadri weaving collective have passed down techniques that can't be found in textbooks. Every knot, every dye bath, and every beat of the loom carries the memory of ancestors and the rhythm of the Western Ghats. Our partnership ensures these traditions remain a living, breathing art form rather than a museum piece.

Using only locally sourced organic cotton and natural indigo harvested during the full moon, each textile represents a minimum of three weeks of mindful creation. The artisans work from open-air verandas overlooking the forest, letting the landscape guide their palette — rich terracotta from the soil, deep ochre from wild turmeric, and the calming cream of raw, unbleached fibers.

Meet the Collective

Made with Meaning, Crafted with Conscience

Every piece in our collection is a testament to time-honored traditions, natural materials, and the dignity of human hands. We partner directly with artisan communities to ensure that beauty never comes at a cost to the earth or its people.

Direct Artisan Partnerships

We work hand-in-hand with over 40 indigenous collectives across South Asia, East Africa, and Latin America. Our fair trade agreements guarantee living wages, safe working conditions, and community reinvestment — because the hands that create deserve as much care as the objects they make.

100% Natural Materials

From hand-spun organic cotton and raw wild silk to unglazed terracotta and sustainably harvested teak, every raw material is traceable to its source. We reject synthetic dyes, petroleum-based fibers, and industrial shortcuts — preserving both the integrity of the craft and the health of the ecosystems that sustain it.

Low-Impact Botanical Dyes

Our signature palette — indigo, madder root, pomegranate rind, and turmeric — is achieved through ancestral dyeing techniques that use 90% less water than conventional methods. Each hue carries the subtle variations of its natural origin, making every piece genuinely one of a kind.
